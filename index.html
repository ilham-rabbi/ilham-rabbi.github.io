Experience

Garton Lab for Synthetic Biology, UofT

GPCR Based Biosensor For Detecting Odorant Stimuli

  • Awarded $7500 NSERC grant to conduct Synthetic Biology research under the supervision of Professor Michael Garton, Synthetic Biology Research Chair of Canada.
  • Worked to develop a novel GPCR-based biosensor with an endogenous stimulation reporter mechanism.
  • Performed PCR amplification and Goldengate cloning to assemble olfactory receptor genes with the genetic components necessary for transducing odorant stimulation to fluorescent signal.
  • Transfected HEK293T derived HANA3A cells with engineered plasmids and culture 14 unique, stable monoclonal and polyclonal cell lines.
  • Verified surface expression and functionality of engineered fusion protein complexes with SDS-PAGE, Western Blot, Immunostaining, and Potassium Assay.

SPARC BioCenter, The Hospital for Sick Children

Improving RAW Mass Spec Data Analysis

  • Deployed a novel comet-based RAW Mass Spec data-processing pipeline for improved proteomics data analysis using both Python Pandas and R.
  • Conducted a comparative study of the existing and new pipelines to determine if the facility should adopt a new data-processing pipeline for client projects.
  • Engaged with investigators at labs, in and outside of the hospital, to present the results of my analyses.
  • Deployed an automated quality control framework to monitor the data quality of four mass spectrometers at all times.
  • Presented study findings at The Hospital for Sick Children SSuRE Symposium and at the University of Toronto Undergraduate Engineering Research Day (UnERD) Conference 2022.
